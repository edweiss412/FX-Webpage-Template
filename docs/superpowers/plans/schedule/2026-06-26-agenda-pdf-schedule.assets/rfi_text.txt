Institutional Investor 11 Annual Redefining Fixed Income Forum
On May 13, 2025, the Redefining Fixed Income Forum will convene in Chicago,
bringing together leading investors from insurance funds and pension plans to
navigate the shifting landscape of fixed income. With the Federal Reserve
initiating interest rate cuts for the first time since 2020, asset allocators are
faced with both opportunities and challenges in an evolving market.
The backdrop of next year's forum is complex: as speculation around inflation
resurges and the health of the US economy remains in question, the low - income
consumer grapples with increasing financial strain. Attendees will delve into
critical discussions surro unding the impact of these economic dynamics on
fixed income portfolios — exploring whether risk spreads will widen, the potential
for a soft or hard landing, and the implications for a possible recession.
Key themes include strategic asset allocation decisions: where to invest amidst
tightening spreads — be it asset - backed, higher - risk, or long - duration assets.
Participants will also assess untapped opportunities in the credit market and
identify which invest ment managers possess the necessary infrastructure,
expertise, and scale to enhance their clients' fixed income returns.
In addition to thought - provoking presentations and panel discussions, the forum
will offer ample networking opportunities for participants to engage with peers,
share insights, and collaborate on innovative investment strategies. The
Redefining Fixed Incom e Forum promises to be an essential gathering for
industry leaders to redefine their approach to fixed income in a transformative
economic climate.

Panel: Asset - Backed Securities: Risks, Rewards, and Strategies
Asset - backed securities (ABS) offer a great opportunity for allocators to diversify risk by investing in a
slice of income streams from various underlying assets. However, the complexity and quality of the
underlying assets can significantly affect the per formance and risk of ABS. This discussion will cover:
• What are some of the more interesting assets backing securities that allocators are
capitalizing on today?
• What are key due diligence factors that allocators and managers are implementing to assess
the credit risk of the underlying assets and the borrowers?
• What are some of the common credit enhancements that allocators are seeing in these
structures today? How are they mitigating risk and what are the complexities of the
structures?
• Are managers tranching new ABS issuances and if so, which tranches are most enticing
among the insurers and pension funds?
• What has performance been the performance in ABS over cycle and are the underlying
assets facing any headwinds that could disrupt credit quality or cash flow?

Proposed Panel: Is the Projected $200 trillion Infrastructure Supercycle Grounded in Fact and Are
Allocators Chomping at the Bit to Invest?
According to some in the asset management world, infrastructure financing demand will surpass $200
trillion over the next three decades for projects focused on digitalization, decarbonization, and
deglobalization. Once considered a more specialized asset c lass, infrastructure is gradually making its
way into the mainstream of institutional portfolios. This panel will explore the opportunities and
complexities of infrastructure debt, including:
• From plain vanilla to high tech, what are the types of infrastructure projects that are seeking
financing?
• What are the different types of debt instruments, vehicles and financing models for these
projects?
• How are cash flows from infrastructure projects modeled, including considerations for
operational stability, regulatory support, and revenue predictability?
• What are the drivers behind this massive investment projection, including policy, technology,
and global trends?
• How are managers navigating credit and default risks?
• What are the main risks that should be thoroughly scrutinized in the due diligence process?
o Creditworthiness of infrastructure project
o Regulatory stability
o P roject execution risks
o Economic cycles

Panel: Bank vs. Non - Bank Loan Allocation s : What Role Does Each Play and How Should They Co -
11:00 – 11:40 AM
Exist in an Institutional Portfolio?
Lakeview Junior Ballroom
Non - bank lending, such as middle market private credit, has been the darling of the investment world
for the past few years, having grown to approximately $1.5T. However, many speculate that the asset
class has yet to be battle tested in a true credit cycle. The US senior loan market is equally as large and
is underwritten according to different parameters , however, it has lagged in attention . The US economy
remains strong, supporting the resilience of both asset classes, but where and when would investors
prefer to be allocated to one versus the other?
• How do the underwriting standards between the two contrast and why ?
• How have each held up during different credit cycles?
• Are either of these asset classes at risk of oversaturation?
• What is the ideal allocation of each within an institutional portfolio and what roles does each
play in terms of risk, return and longevity?
