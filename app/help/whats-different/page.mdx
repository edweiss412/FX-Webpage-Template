# What's different from Sheets

Three small habits change. The rest stays. Your sheet is still the source of truth, and you still edit it the same way. The sections below cover what stays the same, what the app now automates, and the three new habits to pick up.

## Same as before

- **Your show sheet.** Edit it in Google Sheets the way you always have. The app reads from it and never writes back.
- **Your workflow outside the sheet.** COI tracking, hotel rosters, transport, and everything else that lives in other tools keeps living there.
- **Crew comms.** Texts, calls, and last-minute changes still flow through your usual channels.
- **Decisions you make.** The app surfaces things for review. You decide what ships.

## Now automated

- **Sync from the sheet.** The app polls your sheet on a schedule and stages changes for review when something looks off. You stop emailing fresh PDFs after every edit.
- **Crew share-link.** Each show gets one share-link. Crew choose their name from the picker instead of opening the raw sheet.
- **Role-based filtering.** Each crew member sees only what's relevant to them. You stop maintaining one-off filtered views.

## New habits

<Step n={1}>
  **Glance at the dashboard each morning.** Open the admin dashboard, scan Active shows for a
  "Synced" status on every row, and move on. Most days this takes a few seconds.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
  **Handle yellow items when they appear.** A yellow **Changes to review** badge means new sheet
  edits are waiting for your approval before crew sees them. Open the show, look at what changed,
  then Apply or Discard. See [Review queues](/help/admin/review-queues) for what to look for.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
  **Use the share-link instead of sharing sheet URLs.** When a new show goes live, copy the current
  share-link from the Share & access section on the per-show panel. Do not paste the raw Drive sheet
  URL into a group thread as a workaround. Crew see role-filtered data only through the app.
</Step>

The deepest of these is the second one. When you've got a feel for what Apply vs. Discard looks like in practice, the rest of the rhythm falls into place. Start with [Review queues](/help/admin/review-queues).
