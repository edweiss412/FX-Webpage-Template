# What's different from Sheets

Three small habits change. The rest stays. Your sheet is still the source of truth, and you still edit it the same way.

## Same as before

- **Your show sheet.** Edit it in Google Sheets the way you always have. The app reads from it and never writes back.
- **Your workflow outside the sheet.** COI tracking, hotel rosters, transport, and everything else that lives in other tools keeps living there.
- **Crew comms.** Texts, calls, and last-minute changes still flow through your usual channels.
- **Decisions you make.** The app surfaces things for review. You decide what ships.

## Now automated

- **Sync from the sheet.** The app polls your sheet on a schedule and stages changes for review when something looks off. You stop emailing fresh PDFs after every edit.
- **Per-crew links.** Each crew member gets a signed link to their own page. You share one link per person instead of pointing everyone at the same sheet.
- **Role-based filtering.** Each crew member sees only what's relevant to them. You stop maintaining one-off filtered views.

## New habits

<Step n={1}>
**Glance at the dashboard each morning.** Open the admin dashboard, scan Active Shows for a green check on every row, and move on. Most days this takes a few seconds.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
**Handle yellow items when they appear.** A yellow **Review staged changes** badge means new sheet edits are waiting for your approval before crew sees them. Open the show, look at what changed, then Apply or Discard. See [Review queues](/help/admin/review-queues) for what to look for.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
**Use signed links instead of sharing sheet URLs.** When a new show goes live, issue each crew member's link from the show's crew section on the per-show panel; URL distribution to crew runs through a developer-built handoff in v1 while the new sharing model is under development. Do not paste the raw Drive sheet URL into a group thread as a workaround — crew see role-filtered data only through their own signed link.
</Step>

The deepest of these is the second one. When you've got a feel for what Apply vs. Discard looks like in practice, the rest of the rhythm falls into place. Start with [Review queues](/help/admin/review-queues).
