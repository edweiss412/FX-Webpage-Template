# Per-show panel

The per-show panel (`/admin/show/<slug>`) is what one show looks like to the app. You land here from the dashboard, by opening a show row or following a **Changes to review** badge.

What you'll see, top to bottom:

- **Staged-review card.** Only when this show has edits waiting for your review; it sits above everything else.
- **Preview as a crew member.** Open the show through any crew member's eyes.
- **Parse warnings.** Soft signals the app flagged on the latest sync.
- **Crew & share access.** The share-link, the share-token controls, and the crew roster.
- **[Sync-status footer](#sync-health).** A quiet line at the bottom showing how the last sync went.

On a quiet show the staged-review card is absent, and the sections just give you a quick read of what the sheet currently looks like to the app.

<h2 id="staged-review-card">Staged review card</h2>

A staged-review card appears at the top of the panel whenever this show has edits that haven't been Applied yet. What the card shows you:

- **Source eyebrow.** Where the change came from: **Auto sync**, **Drive push**, **Manual sync**, or **Onboarding scan**.
- **Heading.** A one-line summary of what changed.
- **Per-item review.** Each item that needs your decision gets a plain-language description, plus a diff between the prior approved version and the staged one for each affected section (crew, hotels, rooms, and so on).
- **Apply / Discard.** The two buttons that resolve the card.

The card also names the version you're approving: "staged from edits Doug made on …" with the staged modified time, so there's no guessing about which save you're looking at. While the card is present, the three sections below render underneath as informational context.

For a full walkthrough of the Apply versus Discard decision, see [Review queues](/help/admin/review-queues#re-stage).

<h2 id="sync-health">Sync health</h2>

Sync health lives in a quiet footer at the bottom of the show page: a single status line that tells you how the last sync with this show's sheet went. It reads **"Last synced …"** with how long ago when everything is fine, or a plain-language problem label when it isn't (the same labels the dashboard's [Sync status column](/help/admin/dashboard#active-shows) uses). The small **?** next to the line opens a short explanation and links back here.

The page-level actions sit on the right side of the same footer. **Re-sync from Drive** makes the app read the sheet again right now, instead of waiting for the next scheduled poll. Use it when:

- You've just edited the sheet and want the change to land now.
- You're fixing a cell a previous sync complained about.

While it runs, the label flips to "Syncing…".

<Callout type="note">
  On an archived show, re-sync is paused: the button is replaced by a note until you unarchive the
  show.
</Callout>

If syncs keep failing in a row, the most likely cause is in the sheet itself: a renamed tab, a column the app expected that's gone missing, or a row layout it doesn't recognize.

<Step n={1}>Open the sheet directly in Google Drive.</Step>

<Step n={2}>Fix the cell the error points at.</Step>

<Step n={3}>Come back here and tap **Re-sync from Drive**.</Step>

<Callout type="note">
  If the failures don't have an obvious cause in the sheet, send the page link and the error text to
  Eric.
</Callout>

<h2 id="preview-as-crew-member">Preview as a crew member</h2>

A read-only entry point that opens the show through a specific crew member's eyes, exactly as they would see it on their phone. Each crew member parsed from the sheet gets a row with a **Preview as** button; tapping a row drops you onto that person's crew page in impersonation mode with a sticky **Previewing as ...** banner so you never mistake a preview for the real page. For the longer walkthrough see [Preview as crew](/help/admin/preview-as-crew).

## Parse warnings

A list of warnings the app flagged on the most recent sync. These are soft signals, not hard failures. The sync still applied and crew can still see the page. For the catalog of warning meanings and what to do about each one, see [Parse warnings](/help/admin/parse-warnings).

<h2 id="crew-preview-links">Crew section and share access</h2>

The Share & access section shows the **Current share-link** for the show, a **Copy** button, a **Rotate share-token** control, and **Reset picker selections** for cases where someone picked the wrong identity.

Below it, the crew section lists one row per crew member from the sheet, with their name and role. To preview the page as a specific crew member, use the [Preview as a crew member](#preview-as-crew-member) section above. For the deeper sharing workflow follow [Sharing crew links](/help/admin/sharing-links).
