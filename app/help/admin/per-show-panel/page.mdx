# Per-show panel

The per-show panel (`/admin?show=<slug>`) is what one show looks like to the app. It opens as show details over the dashboard when you open a show row or follow a **Changes to review** badge; close it with the X, a tap outside, or Esc (on a phone, swipe it down) and you're back on the dashboard exactly where you left it.

The panel's main parts:

- **Status strip.** Right under the title: the publish toggle, the Live badge when the show is running, the sync status, and the copy-link for the crew URL.
- **Alerts and parse warnings.** Anything the app flagged on the latest sync, surfaced in the Overview section.
- **Crew & share access.** The crew roster (each row has a **⋮ menu** with **Preview as** and **Reset name picker**), the show's share-link, and the share-token controls.
- **Sheet changes.** The show's feed of what synced recently: edits applied on their own, plus anything waiting for your OK. ([Jump](#changes-feed).)
- **[Sync health](#sync-health).** The status strip's quiet sync line, with **Re-sync** beside it in the same strip.

There is no footer bar: publishing lives in the status strip, and archiving is a row in the Overview section. On a quiet show the Sheet changes feed just reads "No changes yet," and the sections give you a quick read of what the sheet currently looks like to the app.

<h2 id="changes-feed">The Sheet changes feed</h2>

Most edits you make to a live show's sheet apply on their own. Each one lands in the show's **Sheet changes** feed, newest first, with a plain-language summary of what changed:

- **Auto-applied edits.** Crew added, removed, or renamed; schedule, field, and diagram or reel changes. Crew see them on their page right away, and most crew add, remove, and rename entries can be reversed with the **Undo this change** button. Auto-applied changes you haven't reviewed yet show an **Accept** button (and **Accept all** in the header); accepted entries keep a quiet **Accepted** tag.
- **A crew member's identity change** (usually an email change, sometimes a rename or removal). This is the one edit that waits for your OK: the entry shows **Approve** and **Reject**, so a wrong or accidental reassignment never goes live on its own. Until you decide, that person's prior details stay in effect.

When nothing has synced recently, the feed reads "No changes yet."

Brand-new sheets are different: a sheet the app has never published waits for your sign-off before it goes live, and you review that one from the [Needs attention inbox](/help/admin/dashboard#pending-ingestion) (see [Review queues](/help/admin/review-queues#first-seen)), not here.

<h2 id="sync-health">Sync health</h2>

Sync health lives in the status strip at the top of the show details: a health dot beside two quiet lines, **"Synced …"** (the last time the app reached the sheet) and **"Edited …"** (the last time the sheet's content changed). When something is off, the line shows a plain-language problem label instead (the same labels the dashboard's [Sync status column](/help/admin/dashboard#active-shows) uses).

The **Re-sync** button sits in the status strip, between the sync line and the copy-link button. It makes the app read the sheet again right now, instead of waiting for the next scheduled poll. Use it when:

- You've just edited the sheet and want the change to land now.
- You're fixing a cell a previous sync complained about.

While it runs, the label flips to "Syncing…" and the button is disabled, so a second tap can't start an overlapping sync. The result appears in a panel just under the strip: a success line, an error, or a confirm asking whether to apply a version that would shrink the show. Success and error panels each have a dismiss button; the shrink confirm is answered with **Keep current version** or **Apply reduced version** instead.

<Callout type="note">
  On an archived show, re-sync is paused: the strip shows no Re-sync button, and the Overview
  section carries a note explaining why, until you unarchive the show.
</Callout>

If syncs keep failing in a row, the most likely cause is in the sheet itself: a renamed tab, a column the app expected that's gone missing, or a row layout it doesn't recognize.

<h2 id="preview-as-crew-member">Preview as a crew member</h2>

A read-only entry point that opens the show through a specific crew member's eyes, exactly as they would see it on their phone. Each crew member parsed from the sheet gets a row; **Preview as** lives in the row's **⋮ menu**, and tapping it drops you onto that person's crew page in impersonation mode with a sticky **Previewing as ...** banner so you never mistake a preview for the real page. For the longer walkthrough see [Preview as crew](/help/admin/preview-as-crew).

## Parse warnings

The alerts area in the Overview section is where the app surfaces any soft signals it flagged on the latest sync. These are not hard failures: the sync still applied and crew can still see the page. For the catalog of warning meanings and what to do about each one, see [Parse warnings](/help/admin/parse-warnings).

<h2 id="crew-preview-links">Crew section and share access</h2>

The Share & access section shows the **Current share-link** for the show, a **Copy** button, a **Rotate share-token** control, and a **Reset everyone's pick** control for cases where the whole show needs a fresh start — to reset one crew member instead, use that row's **⋮ menu** in the crew section.

Below it, the crew section lists one row per crew member from the sheet, with their name and role. To preview the page as a specific crew member, open that row's **⋮ menu** and choose **Preview as** (see the [Preview as a crew member](#preview-as-crew-member) section above). For the deeper sharing workflow follow [Sharing crew links](/help/admin/sharing-links).
