# Reading the dashboard

The admin dashboard is where you start each morning. On a wider screen it's two columns — the **Active shows** table on the left, the **Needs attention** inbox on the right. On a phone they stack, and the inbox collapses to a summary card that opens its own page. On a quiet day, you scan it and move on.

<h2 id="active-shows">The Active shows table</h2>

One row per show. Next to the **Active shows** title sits a count chip with the total number of shows on your account — even if the list below is capped. The **Find** box narrows the list as you type a show name, and the **Active** / **Archived** toggle switches which bucket you're looking at.

The column headers — **Show**, **Dates**, **Crew**, **Sync status** — double as sort buttons: tap one to sort by that column, tap it again to flip the direction. On a phone the columns stack under each show's title instead of spreading across the row. Reading a row:

- **Title and state.** The show name, exactly as it appears in your sheet (long names wrap, never get cut off). A live show carries a **Live** pill next to its title; a show going through its first publish shows **Publishing…**; a show brought back from the archive but not yet republished shows **Held — not published**.
- **Dates.** Load-in through load-out, formatted `3/22/26 → 3/26/26`. Shown as a dash if the sheet didn't have dates yet.
- **Crew.** The count of crew rows the latest applied sync produced.
- **Sync status.** A colored dot plus plain words telling you how the last sync went.

The **Sync status** column is the part that matters most. The values you'll see:

| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Synced** (+ how long ago)                                                            | The sheet polled cleanly; any edits were auto-applied.                                                                                                                                                       | Nothing.                                                                           |
| **⚠ Changes to review**                                                                | Uncommon. A whole sheet for a show that already exists is held for your sign-off (for example, after a re-scan). Routine live-show edits do not land here; they auto-apply into the show's **Changes** feed. | Open it from the [Needs attention inbox](#pending-ingestion).                      |
| **"Couldn't reach Drive"** / **"Sheet not in folder"** / **"Couldn't read the sheet"** | The most recent sync hard-failed. The crew page still serves the last good version.                                                                                                                          | Tap the row, read the plain-language error, fix the sheet, and re-sync from there. |
| **"Sync in progress"**                                                                 | A poll is running right now.                                                                                                                                                                                 | Check back in a moment.                                                            |
| **Not synced yet**                                                                     | The app hasn't completed a poll for this show yet.                                                                                                                                                           | Nothing; just wait for the next poll.                                              |

Tapping anywhere on a row opens that show's per-show panel — that's where every action lives (re-sync, preview as crew, the changes feed, sharing). The table itself is a read-only glance.

The empty state (no shows yet) reads "You do not have any shows yet." and tells you to share a sheet into your watched Drive folder so the sync can pick it up.

<h2 id="pending-ingestion">The Needs attention inbox</h2>

The right-hand column collects everything waiting on you, across all shows, with a count chip showing the total. On a phone it appears as a summary card instead — tap it to open the full **Needs attention** page. Three kinds of cards show up here:

- **Couldn't process a sheet.** A new sheet appeared in your Drive folder, but the app couldn't fetch or read it, so it never became a show. The card names the file and explains what went wrong in plain words. Your options: **Retry now** (the app tries again), **Defer until modified** (the card stays out of the way until the sheet is edited again), or **Permanently ignore** (the card is dismissed for good). A successful retry doesn't skip your sign-off — the sheet moves along the same path as any other new sheet.
- **New sheet to review.** The sheet parsed fine, but it needs your sign-off before going live: it came in through the onboarding wizard's scan, or auto-publish is off. Tap **Review** to open the staged-review surface, where you confirm the show before it goes live or discard it without ever creating a crew link.
- **Changes to review.** Uncommon: a whole sheet for a show that already exists is held for sign-off (for example, after a re-scan). Tap **Open show** to open it. Routine live-show edits are not here; they auto-apply into the show's Changes feed.

A card stays in the inbox until you act on it. Nothing here clears itself. When it's empty it says "Nothing waiting on you." — the best way to read the dashboard.

<h2 id="archived">Archived shows</h2>

The **Archived** side of the bucket toggle lists shows you've archived. Their crew links stay off until you unarchive and republish — crew who open an old link see nothing, which is the point of archiving a wrapped show.

You archive from the bottom of a show's per-show page (**Archive show**). Each row in the archived list has a one-tap **Unarchive**, which moves the show back to the Active list as **Held — not published**. Open it and publish it again to turn the crew links back on.

## Changes and review

Most edits to an already-live show apply on their own and land in that show's **Changes** feed; open the show to see them, and most crew changes can be undone there. The app only holds a change for your sign-off in two cases: a **brand-new sheet** it hasn't published yet (it appears in the **Needs attention** inbox as **New sheet to review**; tap **Review** to Apply or discard it), and a **crew member's identity change** (usually an email), which waits for **Approve** or **Reject** in the show's Changes feed. For the full picture see [Review queues](/help/admin/review-queues).
