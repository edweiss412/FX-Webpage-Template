# Reading the dashboard

The admin dashboard is where you start each morning. Two stacked panels tell you whether anything needs your attention. On a quiet day, you scan it and move on.

<h2 id="active-shows">The Active shows panel</h2>

One row per show. The row is a single line on a wider screen and stacks vertically on a phone. Each row reads from left to right (or top to bottom on a phone):

- **Title.** The show name, exactly as it appears in your sheet. Tapping the title opens the per-show panel for that show.
- **Dates.** Load-in through load-out, formatted `3/22 → 3/26/26`. Hidden if the sheet didn't have dates yet.
- **Crew count.** A short `crew` label preceded by the count of crew rows the latest applied sync produced.
- **Last sync + status.** Relative time since the last poll (e.g., "5 min ago") followed by `·` and one of the status indicators below.

The **status indicator** is the part of the row that matters most. The shipped values are:

- **`✓`** — The sheet polled cleanly and any edits were auto-applied. Nothing for you to do.
- **`⚠ Review staged changes`** — The sheet has new edits that tripped a review rule, so the app staged them instead of auto-applying. The crew still sees the prior approved version. Tap the row's title to open the per-show panel; the staged-review card sits at the top with Apply + Discard. See [Review queues](/help/admin/review-queues#re-stage) for what to do next.
- **`✗ Needs attention`** — The most recent sync hard-failed (parse error, or the sheet was moved or unshared). The crew page still serves the last good version. Tap the title to open the per-show panel, read the error message, fix the sheet, and re-sync from there.
- **`Publishing…`** — The show is going through its first publish after onboarding. Show up shortly as `✓`.
- **`·`** — Quiet placeholder when the sync state isn't one of the above (e.g., a never-synced row, or a status string the panel doesn't have a dedicated glyph for).

Every row-level action — opening the show, re-syncing from Drive, previewing as a crew member, reviewing staged changes — lives one click deeper, on the per-show panel that opens when you tap the title. The dashboard row is read-only at a glance; the per-show panel is where you act.

The empty state (no shows yet) reads "You do not have any shows yet." and tells you to share a sheet into your watched Drive folder so the sync can pick it up.

<h2 id="pending-ingestion">The Sheets-we-couldn't-auto-apply panel</h2>

Most new sheets in your folder publish on their own and show up in **Active shows**. The confirmation email carries a 24-hour one-click undo link for mistakes. This panel only fills up when the app couldn't take a sheet that far on its own. Two kinds of rows show up here:

- **First-seen sheets that hard-failed.** A new sheet appeared in your Drive folder, but the fetch or parse failed before it could become a show. Each row shows the file name, when it was first seen, how many times the app has tried, and the error message. Your options are **Retry now** (so the app tries again), **Defer until modified** (the row stays out of the way until the sheet's modified time changes), and **Permanently ignore** (the row is dismissed for good). Retry never auto-applies a first-seen sheet, even when everything passes. It can only move the row into the queue below, where you Apply it explicitly.
- **First-seen sheets held for review.** The parse passed, but something tripped a review rule (a crew count that shrank, a LEAD status that toggled, an email that moved between rows, a diagram tab that came back empty), or the sheet was surfaced by the onboarding wizard's scan step. Either way the app held it for your sign-off. Each row shows the candidate title and dates the app pulled out, plus a list of what tripped the review. Your options are **Review and apply** (opens the staged-review surface, where you confirm the show goes live) and **Discard** (drops the candidate without ever creating a public crew link).

A row stays in this panel until you act on it. Nothing here clears itself.

## Staged-changes review

Edits to an already-live show don't appear here. They show up as a yellow **Review staged changes** badge on the show's row in the Active shows panel above. The handling is the same idea, just in a different place. For the full walkthrough see [Review queues](/help/admin/review-queues#re-stage).
