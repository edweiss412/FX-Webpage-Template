# Review queues

Most new sheets in your Drive folder publish on their own. Dropping a clean sheet into the folder is the deliberate publish action; the app parses it, mints a show row, and crew can see the page right away.

Two kinds of change need your attention, and they live in different places:

| Where to look                        | What it is                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **[First-seen review](#first-seen)** | A brand-new sheet the app hasn't published yet, held for your sign-off (from the onboarding scan, or when auto-publish is off). It waits in the **Needs attention** inbox until you Apply or Discard it. |
| **[Live-show changes](#re-stage)**   | Edits to a show that's already live. Most apply on their own and show in that show's **Changes** feed; a crew member's identity change (usually an email) waits for your Approve or Reject there.        |

First-seen items are durable: they wait in the **Needs attention** panel (right column of the dashboard, next to **Active shows**) until you act. No sheets are waiting in the captured state below, so the panel is showing the empty message you will see once every review item has been cleared.

<h2 id="first-seen">First-seen review</h2>

A first-seen sheet is one the app has never published. With **auto-publish** on, a clean new sheet publishes on its own and appears in Active shows right away. A sheet lands here for your sign-off only when:

- it came in through the **onboarding wizard's scan**, which always routes new sheets to you before activating sync, or
- **auto-publish is off** (a Settings toggle), so every new sheet waits for your explicit OK.

A sheet the app couldn't read at all is a different case: it shows up as a "couldn't process a sheet" card in the [Needs attention inbox](/help/admin/dashboard#pending-ingestion).

You'll find first-seen rows in the **Needs attention** panel on the dashboard, each labeled **New sheet to review** with a **Review** link to the staged-review surface, where the items that need your decision are listed.

On that surface you either:

- **Apply** the sheet, which mints the show row and publishes the crew page. A crew link exists from that moment forward.
- Discard it instead, choosing **Retry on next sync**, **Wait for next edit**, or **Stop showing this sheet**. No crew link is ever created.

<Callout type="warning">
  Discard is one-way for the staged candidate, but it does not touch your sheet. The sheet in Drive
  is fine. If you Discard and then change your mind, edit the sheet (even a trivial edit is enough)
  and the next sync will surface it again as a fresh first-seen candidate.
</Callout>

<h2 id="re-stage">Live-show changes</h2>

When you edit a show that's already live, most changes apply on the next sync and land in that show's **Changes** feed on the [per-show panel](/help/admin/per-show-panel#changes-feed), newest first, so crew see them right away. The one exception, a crew member's identity change, the app holds for your decision (see below).

What lands in the feed:

- **Crew added, removed, or renamed.** Applied automatically; most entries offer an **Undo this change** button if you need to walk one back.
- **Schedule, field, and diagram or reel changes.** Applied automatically and logged, so you can see what moved.
- **A crew member's identity change** (usually an email). The one edit that holds for your decision: the entry shows **Approve** and **Reject**, and that person's prior details stay in effect until you choose.

Because routine edits are already live, you act on individual feed entries (**Undo this change**, or **Approve** / **Reject**), not a whole-sync Apply or Discard.

## Reviewing a first-seen sheet: Apply or Discard?

When you open a first-seen sheet from the inbox, you Apply it (which publishes the show) or Discard it:

| Apply when                                             | Discard when                                                          |
| ------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| The change is intentional and you want crew to see it. | You spot a typo, a paste accident, or a row you didn't mean to touch. |
| The items shown match what you expect from the sheet.  | A crew row vanished and you can't account for it.                     |
| The diff next to each item lines up with the sheet.    | You started a sheet you aren't ready to publish yet.                  |

<Callout type="tip">
  Not sure? **Discard** is the safer choice: the sheet stays put, and the next sync surfaces it
  again as a fresh first-seen candidate. If something was a mistake, fix the sheet first.
</Callout>
