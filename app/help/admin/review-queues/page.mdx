# Review queues

Most new sheets in your Drive folder publish on their own. Dropping a clean sheet into the folder is the deliberate publish action; the app parses it, mints a show row, and crew can see the page right away.

Two queues catch the sheets the app can't publish on its own:

| Queue                         | Catches                                                                                                              |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **[First-seen](#first-seen)** | A brand-new sheet held for your sign-off before it goes live: from the onboarding scan, or when auto-publish is off. |
| **[Re-stage](#re-stage)**     | Edits to an already-live show that tripped a review rule.                                                            |

Both queues are durable. Nothing here clears itself.

Both queues surface in one place on the dashboard: the **Needs attention** panel, in the right column next to **Active shows**. No sheets are waiting in the captured state below, so the panel is showing the empty message you will see after every review item has been cleared.

<h2 id="first-seen">First-seen review</h2>

A first-seen sheet is one the app has never published. With **auto-publish** on, a clean new sheet publishes on its own and appears in Active shows right away. A sheet lands here for your sign-off only when:

- it came in through the **onboarding wizard's scan**, which always routes new sheets to you before activating sync, or
- **auto-publish is off** (a Settings toggle), so every new sheet waits for your explicit OK.

A sheet the app couldn't read at all is a different case: it shows up as a "couldn't process a sheet" card in the [Needs attention inbox](/help/admin/dashboard#pending-ingestion).

You'll find first-seen review rows in the **Needs attention** panel on the dashboard. Each row shows the candidate title and a **Review** link that opens the staged-review surface, where the items that need your decision are listed.

The **Review** link opens the staged-review surface, where two actions are available:

- **Apply.** Confirm each decision, then Apply mints the show row and publishes the crew page. A crew link exists from that moment forward.
- **Discard.** Drops the candidate. No crew link is ever created.

<Callout type="warning">
  Discard is one-way for the staged candidate, but it does not touch your sheet. The sheet in Drive
  is fine. If you Discard and then change your mind, edit the sheet (even a trivial edit is enough)
  and the next sync will surface it again as a fresh first-seen candidate.
</Callout>

<h2 id="re-stage">Re-stage review</h2>

A re-stage happens when you edit a show that's already live and the edit tripped a review rule. The app catches it, holds the new version in staging, and keeps serving the prior approved version to crew. The crew page does not flicker, and stale data does not go out.

Re-stage rows also appear in the **Needs attention** panel, marked **Changes to review**. The **Open show** link takes you straight to the per-show parse panel with the staged review at the top.

Common triggers:

- Crew row count dropped from one sync to the next, or a crew identity drifted.
- A schedule note, lead role, or position assignment changed in a way the app wants you to confirm.
- An email on the crew block changed, was reassigned, or moved between rows.
- A diagram or opening-reel reference changed since the last published version.

Apply and Discard work the same way as the first-seen flow. Apply replaces the live version with the staged one. Discard drops the staged version and leaves the prior approved version live.

## Apply or Discard?

| Apply when                                                  | Discard when                                                          |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| The change is intentional and you want crew to see it.      | You spot a typo, a paste accident, or a row you didn't mean to touch. |
| The triggered review items match edits you remember making. | A crew row vanished and you can't account for it.                     |
| The diff next to each item lines up with the sheet.         | You started an edit you aren't ready to publish yet.                  |

<Callout type="tip">
  Not sure? **Discard** is the safer choice: the sheet stays put and the next sync surfaces the same
  review again. If the change was a mistake, fix the sheet first so it re-stages cleanly.
</Callout>
