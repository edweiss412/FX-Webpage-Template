# Review queues

Most new sheets in your Drive folder publish on their own. Dropping a clean sheet into the folder is the deliberate publish action; the app parses it, mints a show row, and crew can see the page right away. If you ever drop the wrong sheet, the confirmation email you receive carries a one-click 24-hour unpublish button as a safety net.

Two queues exist for the cases where the app cannot publish on its own. The first-seen queue catches brand-new sheets that the app held for your sign-off because something in the parse looked unusual, or that hard-failed before they could become a show. The re-stage queue catches edits to a show that's already live, when those edits tripped a review rule.

Both queues are durable. Nothing here clears itself.

<h2 id="first-seen">First-seen review</h2>

A first-seen sheet is one the app has never published. Most first-seen sheets in your watched folder publish on their own, and you see them in the Active Shows panel right away (with the 24-hour email-undo for mistakes). A first-seen sheet only lands in this queue when the parse tripped a review rule (a crew count that shrank unexpectedly, a LEAD status that toggled, an email that moved between rows, a diagram tab that came back empty, and so on), or when you discovered the sheet through the onboarding wizard's scan step (which always routes to you before activating sync).

You'll find first-seen review rows in the **Sheets we couldn't auto-apply** panel on the dashboard. Each row shows the candidate title and dates the parser pulled out of the sheet, plus the list of items that need your decision.

Two actions are available:

- **Review and apply.** Opens the staged-review surface. You confirm each decision, then Apply mints the show row and publishes the crew page. A crew link exists from that moment forward.
- **Discard.** Drops the candidate. No crew link is ever created.

<Callout type="warning">
Discard is one-way for the staged candidate, but it does not touch your sheet. The sheet in Drive is fine. If you Discard and then change your mind, edit the sheet (even a trivial edit is enough) and the next sync will surface it again as a fresh first-seen candidate.
</Callout>

<h2 id="re-stage">Re-stage review</h2>

A re-stage happens when you edit a show that's already live and the edit tripped a review rule. The app catches it, holds the new version in staging, and keeps serving the prior approved version to crew. The crew page does not flicker, and stale data does not go out.

Re-stage rows don't show up in the **Sheets we couldn't auto-apply** panel. They appear as a yellow **Review staged changes** badge on the affected show's row in the **Active Shows** panel. The row's primary action becomes **Review staged changes**, which takes you straight to the per-show parse panel with the staged review at the top.

Common triggers:

- Crew row count dropped from one sync to the next, or a crew identity drifted.
- A schedule note, lead role, or position assignment changed in a way the parser wants you to confirm.
- An email on the crew block changed, was reassigned, or moved between rows.
- A diagram or opening-reel reference changed since the last published version.

Apply and Discard work the same way as the first-seen flow. Apply replaces the live version with the staged one. Discard drops the staged version and leaves the prior approved version live.

## When to Apply

- The change in the sheet is intentional and you want crew to see it.
- The triggered review items match edits you remember making (or that Doug told you about).
- The diff shown next to each item lines up with the sheet.

## When to Discard

- You spot a typo, a paste accident, or a row you didn't mean to touch. Fix the sheet first, then let the next sync re-stage cleanly.
- A crew row vanished and you can't account for it. Discard, investigate, edit the sheet, and let the app re-stage on the corrected version.
- You started an edit you aren't ready to publish. Discard the staged version; the prior approved version keeps serving crew.

If you're not sure, Discard is the safer of the two. The sheet stays put. The next sync will surface the same review again.
