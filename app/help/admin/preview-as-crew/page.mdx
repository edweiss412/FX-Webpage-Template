# Preview as crew

**See any show through a crew member's eyes: exactly what they see on their phone, without sending a link or changing their own page.**

Start from the [Preview as a crew member section](/help/admin/per-show-panel#preview-as-crew-member) on the per-show panel. Each row there is one crew member, and the **Preview as** action drops you onto their crew page in impersonation mode.

Reach for it when you want to:

- Confirm a fix landed for a specific person.
- Spot-check role-based filtering before a tour day starts.
- Sanity-check call times and hotel info after a sheet edit.

<h2 id="impersonation-banner">The impersonation banner</h2>

While you're impersonating a crew member, a sticky yellow banner sits along the top of the page: the **Previewing as** eyebrow, the crew member's name (**Previewing as Alex Rivera**, for example), their role pill, and an **Exit preview** button. It stays pinned as you scroll, so you can't forget you're in preview mode.

Two ways out:

- **Tap Exit preview.** Drops you back to the per-show panel.
- **Close the tab.** Impersonation is scoped to the current browser session and never persists.

This banner is also the target of the small `?` icon you see on the crew page itself when previewing. Tapping the `?` brings you to this section directly.

## What to verify

Walk the page top to bottom with the specific crew member in mind:

- The **call time** at the top matches what you expect for this person's role on this show date. If it looks off, the fix is almost always in the sheet (a typo in the call column, a wrong row for this person), not in the app.
- **Hotel and room info** is present for crew members who have hotel rows, and absent for day-of locals who don't.
- **Role-restricted information** is hidden where it should be. Stagehands shouldn't see tour-manager-only logistics, and the hidden fields are the most common thing to verify with this tool.
- **Schedule items, contact info, and notes** read cleanly on a phone-sized viewport. Resize the browser narrow if you're on desktop.

## Why some fields are hidden

The crew page filters what each crew member sees by role. A stagehand sees their own call, hotel, schedule, and the contacts they need, and nothing more; a tour manager sees the full picture. This is by design, matching how you already handle the printed run sheets.

<Callout type="tip">
  **Hidden on purpose, or missing?** If a field is hidden here but shows when you preview the same
  show as a **tour manager**, it's correctly role-restricted. If it's missing in **every** preview
  for the show, it's missing from the sheet, and the fix lives there.
</Callout>
