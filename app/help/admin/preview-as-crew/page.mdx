# Preview as crew

Preview as crew lets you open any show through a crew member's eyes, exactly as they would see it on their phone, without sending them a link and without changing what they see on their own page. You launch the preview from the [crew preview links list on the per-show panel](/help/admin/per-show-panel#crew-preview-links): each row there is one crew member, and tapping a row drops you onto their crew page in impersonation mode. Use this to confirm a fix landed for a specific person, to spot-check role-based filtering before a tour day starts, or to sanity-check call times and hotel info after a sheet edit.

<h2 id="impersonation-banner">The impersonation banner</h2>

While you're impersonating a crew member, a sticky yellow banner sits along the top of the page with the crew member's name (**Previewing as Alex Rivera**, for example) and a clearly labeled **Exit preview** button on the right. The banner stays pinned as you scroll, so you cannot accidentally forget you're in preview mode. It pairs the yellow fill with the **Previewing as** eyebrow, the crew member's name, the role label pill, and the **Exit preview** button so the state is legible without relying on color alone. Tap **Exit preview** to drop back to the per-show panel; closing the tab works too, since impersonation is scoped to the current browser session and never persists.

This banner is also the target of the small `?` icon you see on the crew page itself when previewing. Tapping the `?` brings you to this section directly.

## What to verify

Walk the page top to bottom with the specific crew member in mind:

- The **call time** at the top matches what you expect for this person's role on this show date. If it looks off, the fix is almost always in the sheet (a typo in the call column, a wrong row for this person), not in the app.
- **Hotel and room info** is present for crew members who have hotel rows, and absent for day-of locals who don't.
- **Role-restricted information** is hidden where it should be. Stagehands shouldn't see tour-manager-only logistics, and the hidden fields are the most common thing to verify with this tool.
- **Schedule items, contact info, and notes** read cleanly on a phone-sized viewport. Resize the browser narrow if you're on desktop.

## Why some fields are hidden

The crew page filters what each crew member sees based on their role. A stagehand opens the page and sees their own call, their own hotel, their own schedule, and the contact info they need, and nothing more. A tour manager opens the same show and sees the full picture. This is by design and matches how Doug already handles the printed run sheets.

If a field looks missing during preview, that is the role filter doing its job, not a bug. The fastest way to confirm is to preview the same show as a tour manager and compare: anything that shows up there but not in the original preview is correctly role-restricted. If a field is missing across every preview for the show, then it is missing from the sheet and the fix lives there.
