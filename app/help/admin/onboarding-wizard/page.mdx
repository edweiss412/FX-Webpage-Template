# Onboarding wizard

The first time you open `/admin` on a brand-new install (no folder configured, no shows yet), the app shows a three-step wizard instead of the dashboard. It walks you from "I have a Google Sheet" to "my crew can see their pages."

<Callout type="note">
  You only see the wizard once per setup. After it succeeds, `/admin` shows the usual dashboard. To
  point the app at a different folder later, start a fresh run from `/admin/settings` `→` Re-run
  setup; the live folder keeps running until the new wizard finalizes.
</Callout>

The three steps below are what the wizard asks you to do, in order. Each step has a `?` tooltip on the screen itself that links back to the matching section here for the longer explanation.

<h2 id="service-account">Step 1: Share your show folder</h2>

The wizard opens on a single screen showing a long-looking email address. That email is the app's identity inside your Google Drive. It can only see folders you explicitly share with it, and it only looks at the one folder you pick. There's no broader access.

What you do on this step:

<Step n={1}>
  **Find the Drive folder where you keep your show sheets, or make a new one.** Anything inside this
  folder is fair game for the app to read; anything outside it is invisible.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
  **Click Share on the folder, paste the service-account email shown on the wizard, and give it
  Viewer access.** Viewer is enough; the app never writes to your Drive.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
  **Come back to the wizard tab and click "I've shared the folder."** That moves you to step 2.
</Step>

If you want to know more about the email itself, the wizard has a small "What's this email?" disclosure inline; it's the same explanation as the first paragraph of this section.

<Callout type="note">
  A **Start over** link sits at the bottom of every wizard step. Tap it to purge any leftover state
  and begin a fresh run (handy if you closed the tab mid-flow). It disappears once onboarding
  succeeds; after that, the equivalent reset is `/admin/settings` `→` Re-run setup.
</Callout>

<h2 id="step-2">Step 2: Verify access</h2>

The second screen asks for the folder URL and runs a one-shot check that the app can actually see it.

What you do on this step:

<Step n={1}>
  **Paste the folder URL into the input labeled "Folder link."** Drive folder URLs look like
  `https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/...`. Grab the URL from your browser's address bar while
  the folder is open in Drive.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
  **Click the Verify and scan button.** While the scan runs the wizard shows "Looking through your
  folder…" plus an elapsed-seconds counter, and the button label flips to "Verifying…" so you know
  it is in flight. Large folders can take a minute.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
  **Review the scan summary, then click "Continue to Step 3."** The summary counts what the scan
  found in the folder.
</Step>

The scan summary breaks the folder down into:

- Sheets ready for review
- Sheets that couldn't be parsed
- Non-sheet files skipped
- Live-row conflicts

If verification doesn't succeed, one of these is why:

- **"That doesn't look like a Google Drive folder URL."** What you pasted isn't a Drive folder link. Drive folder URLs look like `https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/...`. Grab the URL from your browser's address bar while the folder is open in Drive, not a link copied from somewhere else.
- **"We can't see this folder yet."** The folder exists, but the service-account email doesn't have access to it. Go back to Drive, double-check that you shared the folder with the email exactly as shown in step 1, and try again. The most common cause is sharing a different folder by accident, or pasting the email into a "Notify people" field instead of the "Share" field.

<Callout type="warning">
  Rarely, the app's own setup is off and you'll see "Something is wrong on our end. The developer
  has been notified." There's nothing to fix on your side; the wizard waits until it's resolved.
</Callout>

<h2 id="step-3">Step 3: Review and publish your sheets</h2>

After verification, the wizard scans the whole folder and shows you every sheet it found, one card per sheet. This is the only step where you do meaningful work; steps 1 and 2 are setup.

Each clean sheet gets an inline review card. The card opens collapsed with a quick summary (show title, client, dates, and counts of crew, rooms, hotels, and schedule days). Expand a card to see the breakdown the app pulled from the sheet, so you can confirm the parse before anything goes live.

Every clean card has a publish checkbox. Ticking it means "make this show live now." A **Select all** control at the top of the list ticks every clean card at once, and a running count shows how many you have selected.

A clean sheet has two outcomes, set by that one checkbox:

| Your choice on a clean card | What happens                                                                                                             |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Tick the publish checkbox   | The show goes live the moment you finish setup. Crew can open it right away.                                             |
| Leave it unticked           | The show is still created, but stays a draft. You will find it later under **Unpublished**, ready to publish in one tap. |

Sheets the app could not read sit in a separate **Needs your attention** group below the clean cards. Each one offers **Retry now** (re-read after you fix the cell in Drive), **Defer until modified** (re-check on the next change), or **Permanently ignore** (no show is created and the sync skips the sheet for good; it moves to the **Ignored sheets** view, where you can bring it back anytime). You have to clear that group before you can finish, so setup is never a dead end.

What you do on this step:

<Step n={1}>
  **Skim each card, expanding the ones you want to double-check.** The summary is enough for a sheet
  you trust; expand to confirm crew, schedule, rooms, and hotels when you want a closer look.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
  **Tick the sheets you want live now.** Use the publish checkbox on each card, or **Select all** to
  tick them in one go. Leave a card unticked to keep it as a draft instead.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
  **Clear the Needs your attention group.** Retry the sheets you can fix, and defer or permanently
  ignore the rest. Once that group is empty, you are free to finish.
</Step>

<Step n={4}>
  **Click "Publish N shows & finish setup."** This creates every show, publishes the ones you
  ticked, and points cron at the folder for ongoing sync. If you left clean sheets unticked, the
  wizard asks you to confirm first, since those become drafts under **Unpublished**. From that
  moment on, the dashboard at `/admin` replaces the wizard, and your day-to-day work happens there.
</Step>

If a sheet fails to publish during finish (the most common cause is someone editing it in Drive at the wrong moment), the wizard tells you which one and gives you a recovery page to try again. The rest of the batch still goes through; you don't have to start over.

<h2 id="unpublished">Unpublished shows</h2>

Every clean sheet you left unticked during setup becomes a draft show, created and quietly syncing but not yet visible to crew. You will find these on the **Unpublished** page, a top-nav route of its own (so it never skews your Active and Archived counts). Each row has a one-tap **Publish** that makes the show live and issues a crew link, exactly as if you had ticked it during setup. Nothing expires here; a draft waits until you publish it.

<h2 id="ignored-sheets">Ignored sheets</h2>

A sheet you **Permanently ignore** (during setup or later review) leaves the wizard with no show created, and the sync skips it on every future scan. The **Ignored sheets** page lists each one by name, when it was ignored, and by whom. A per-row **Un-ignore** brings the sheet back: on the next folder scan it returns as a fresh sheet you can review and publish.
