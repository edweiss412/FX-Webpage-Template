# Onboarding wizard

The first time you open `/admin` on a brand-new install (no folder configured, no shows yet), the app shows a three-step wizard instead of the dashboard. It walks you from "I have a Google Sheet" to "my crew can see their pages."

<Callout type="note">
  You only see the wizard once per setup. After it succeeds, `/admin` shows the usual dashboard. To
  point the app at a different folder later, start a fresh run from `/admin/settings` `→` Re-run
  setup; the live folder keeps running until the new wizard finalizes.
</Callout>

The three steps below are what the wizard asks you to do, in order. Each step has a `?` tooltip on the screen itself that links back to the matching section here for the longer explanation.

<h2 id="service-account">Step 1: Share your show folder</h2>

The wizard opens on a single screen showing a long-looking email address. That email is the app's identity inside your Google Drive. It can only see folders you explicitly share with it, and it only looks at the one folder you pick. There's no broader access.

What you do on this step:

<Step n={1}>
  **Find the Drive folder where you keep your show sheets, or make a new one.** Anything inside this
  folder is fair game for the app to read; anything outside it is invisible.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
  **Click Share on the folder, paste the service-account email shown on the wizard, and give it
  Viewer access.** Viewer is enough; the app never writes to your Drive.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
  **Come back to the wizard tab and click "I've shared the folder."** That moves you to step 2.
</Step>

If you want to know more about the email itself, the wizard has a small "What's this email?" disclosure inline; it's the same explanation as the first paragraph of this section.

<Callout type="note">
  A **Start over** link sits at the bottom of every wizard step. Tap it to purge any leftover state
  and begin a fresh run (handy if you closed the tab mid-flow). It disappears once onboarding
  succeeds; after that, the equivalent reset is `/admin/settings` `→` Re-run setup.
</Callout>

<h2 id="step-2">Step 2: Verify access</h2>

The second screen asks for the folder URL and runs a one-shot check that the app can actually see it.

What you do on this step:

<Step n={1}>
  **Paste the folder URL into the input labeled "Folder link."** Drive folder URLs look like
  `https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/...`. Grab the URL from your browser's address bar while
  the folder is open in Drive.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
  **Click the Verify and scan button.** While the scan runs the wizard shows "Looking through your
  folder…" plus an elapsed-seconds counter, and the button label flips to "Verifying…" so you know
  it is in flight. Large folders can take a minute.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
  **Review the scan summary, then click "Continue to Step 3."** The summary counts what the scan
  found in the folder.
</Step>

The scan summary breaks the folder down into:

- Sheets ready for review
- Sheets that couldn't be parsed
- Non-sheet files skipped
- Live-row conflicts

If verification doesn't succeed, one of these is why:

- **"That doesn't look like a Google Drive folder URL."** What you pasted isn't a Drive folder link. Drive folder URLs look like `https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/...`. Grab the URL from your browser's address bar while the folder is open in Drive, not a link copied from somewhere else.
- **"We can't see this folder yet."** The folder exists, but the service-account email doesn't have access to it. Go back to Drive, double-check that you shared the folder with the email exactly as shown in step 1, and try again. The most common cause is sharing a different folder by accident, or pasting the email into a "Notify people" field instead of the "Share" field.

<Callout type="warning">
  Rarely, the app's own setup is off and you'll see "Something is wrong on our end. The developer
  has been notified." There's nothing to fix on your side; the wizard waits until it's resolved.
</Callout>

<h2 id="step-3">Step 3: First sheets review</h2>

After verification, the wizard scans the whole folder and shows you every sheet it found, one row per sheet. This is the only step where you do meaningful work; steps 1 and 2 are setup.

Each row carries a status badge. You start with three first-contact statuses and add a fourth as soon as you've acted on a row; the resolved statuses below replace the first-contact one in place once you've made the call.

First-contact statuses:

| Badge                           | What it means                                                                                                | Your options                                                                                                                                                         |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Ready for review**            | The sheet parsed cleanly.                                                                                    | **Review and apply** to walk through what the app extracted and approve it.                                                                                          |
| **Couldn't parse**              | A column wasn't recognized, or a date cell didn't read as a date (the badge shows the plain-English reason). | **Retry now** (re-read after you fix the cell in Drive) · **Defer until modified** (re-check on the next change) · **Permanently ignore** (stays out of the system). |
| **Skipped: not a Google Sheet** | The folder held a non-Sheet file (a doc, a PDF, an image).                                                   | Nothing; informational, auto-resolved.                                                                                                                               |
| **Live row conflict**           | (Rare recovery) the same sheet already exists in your live data, so the wizard won't promote it again.       | Resolve the live row from the dashboard, then re-run setup.                                                                                                          |

After you act on a row, its badge flips to a resolved state:

| Resolved badge                      | Meaning                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Applied**                         | The sheet has been promoted.                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **Deferred until modified**         | We'll re-check on the next change.                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| **Permanently ignored**             | The sheet stays out of the system.                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| **Set aside (try again next sync)** | You discarded a staged change with the "try again next sync" option. **This is the one resolved-looking badge that still needs another decision** before you can finish setup; the wizard lists which rows are still pending in the count above the list. |

What you do on this step:

<Step n={1}>
  **Open each row and review what the app extracted.** "Parsed and ready" rows are the ones that
  need your eyeballs; the other statuses are informational.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
  **Approve or discard each row.** Approving stages the sheet for promotion; discarding has three
  flavors (try again next sync, defer until the sheet changes, or permanently ignore). The wizard
  will not let you finish until every row reaches a resolved state.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
  **Click Finalize once every row is resolved.** This promotes every approved sheet into the live
  system, makes them visible to crew, and points cron at the folder for ongoing sync. From that
  moment on, the dashboard at `/admin` replaces the wizard, and your day-to-day work happens there.
</Step>

If something goes wrong during finalize (the most common cause is someone editing one of the sheets in Drive between approval and finalize), the wizard tells you which specific rows failed and gives you a link to re-approve them. The rest of the batch still goes through; you don't have to start over.
