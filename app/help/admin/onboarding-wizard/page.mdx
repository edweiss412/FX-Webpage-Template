# Onboarding wizard

The first time you open `/admin` on a brand-new install, the app shows a three-step wizard instead of the usual dashboard. The wizard walks you from "I have a Google Sheet" to "the app is showing my crew their pages." You only see it once per setup; most of the time you'll never come back to this page. It exists for the two times you might: the first run, and a future re-run if you ever start over from scratch.

You'll see the wizard automatically when the app has no folder configured and no shows in the database. After onboarding succeeds, the wizard disappears from `/admin` and the usual dashboard takes its place. If you ever need to point the app at a different Drive folder, you start a new wizard run from `/admin/settings` `→` Re-run setup; the live folder keeps running until the new wizard finalizes.

The three steps below are what the wizard asks you to do, in order. Each step has a `?` tooltip on the screen itself that links back to the matching section here for the longer explanation.

<h2 id="service-account">Step 1: Share your show folder</h2>

The wizard opens on a single screen showing a long-looking email address. That email is the app's identity inside your Google Drive. It can only see folders you explicitly share with it, and it only looks at the one folder you pick. There's no broader access.

What you do on this step:

<Step n={1}>
**Find the Drive folder where you keep your show sheets, or make a new one.** Anything inside this folder is fair game for the app to read; anything outside it is invisible.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
**Click Share on the folder, paste the service-account email shown on the wizard, and give it Viewer access.** Viewer is enough; the app never writes to your Drive.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
**Come back to the wizard tab and click "I've shared the folder."** That moves you to step 2.
</Step>

If you want to know more about the email itself, the wizard has a small "What's this email?" disclosure inline; it's the same explanation as the first paragraph of this section.

A "Start over" link sits at the bottom of every wizard step while you're in onboarding mode. Tap it if you closed the tab mid-flow yesterday and want a fresh run with no leftover state. The link disappears once onboarding succeeds; after that, the equivalent reset lives in `/admin/settings` `→` Re-run setup.

<h2 id="step-2">Step 2: Verify access</h2>

The second screen asks for the folder URL (or just the folder ID) and checks in real time that the app can actually see it.

What you do on this step:

<Step n={1}>
**Paste the folder URL (or just the folder ID) into the wizard's input.** The app checks access in real time; you don't click a "Verify" button separately.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
**When you see the green check with the folder name and sheet count, click the button to move to step 3.** The screen reads something like "Found N sheets in your folder, ready to bring them in?"
</Step>

If the check doesn't come back green, one of these is why:

- **"That doesn't look like a Google Drive folder URL."** What you pasted isn't a Drive folder link. Drive folder URLs look like `https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/...`. Grab the URL from your browser's address bar while the folder is open in Drive, not a link copied from somewhere else.
- **"We can't see this folder yet."** The folder exists, but the service-account email doesn't have access to it. Go back to Drive, double-check that you shared the folder with the email exactly as shown in step 1, and try again. The most common cause is sharing a different folder by accident, or pasting the email into a "Notify people" field instead of the "Share" field.

<Callout type="warning">
There's a fourth, rare outcome where the app's own credentials are misconfigured. If that happens, the wizard tells you "Something is wrong on our end. The developer has been notified." That's an Eric-side problem, not a Doug-side problem; the wizard will not move forward until it's fixed, and you don't have to do anything from your end.
</Callout>

The wizard never shows raw error codes on this step. If a screen ever does, that's a bug, not a feature.

<h2 id="step-3">Step 3: First sheets review</h2>

After verification, the wizard scans the whole folder and shows you every sheet it found, one row per sheet. This is the only step where you do meaningful work; steps 1 and 2 are setup.

Each row has one of four statuses:

- **Parsed and ready.** Green check. Click to review and approve. The sheet looks like a valid show sheet and the app pulled out crew, schedule, hotel, transportation, and everything else the way it expects. Approve once you've eyeballed it.
- **Couldn't parse.** Yellow warning with a plain-English explanation of what went wrong. Most of the time this is "the sheet has a column we don't recognize" or "a date cell didn't read as a date." Click to see the details. You can fix the sheet in Drive and re-run the scan, or defer the sheet (it'll be re-checked the next time it changes).
- **Skipped.** Gray badge. The folder contained something that isn't a Google Sheet (a doc, a PDF, an image). The app ignores it; you don't need to do anything.
- **Already in live sync.** Red warning. This only happens in an unusual recovery situation where the same sheet exists in your live data already; the wizard won't promote it again. You resolve the live row from the dashboard, then re-run the wizard.

What you do on this step:

<Step n={1}>
**Open each row and review what the parser extracted.** "Parsed and ready" rows are the ones that need your eyeballs; the other statuses are informational.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
**Approve or discard each row.** Approving stages the sheet for promotion; discarding has three flavors (try again next sync, defer until the sheet changes, or permanently ignore). The wizard will not let you finish until every row reaches a resolved state.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
**Click Finalize once every row is resolved.** This promotes every approved sheet into the live system, makes them visible to crew, and points cron at the folder for ongoing sync. From that moment on, the dashboard at `/admin` replaces the wizard, and your day-to-day work happens there.
</Step>

If something goes wrong during finalize (the most common cause is someone editing one of the sheets in Drive between approval and finalize), the wizard tells you which specific rows failed and gives you a link to re-approve them. The rest of the batch still goes through; you don't have to start over.

If you ever want to abandon a wizard run completely, the "Start over" link at the bottom of any step purges all the staged state and drops you back at step 1. After onboarding succeeds, the equivalent action is "Re-run setup" in `/admin/settings`.
